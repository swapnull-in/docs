## Overview

**Churn risk** (in the left nav) surfaces customers a scoring model predicts are
likely to cancel. The page has two sections:

- **Open alerts** — cards raised when a customer's churn score spikes past a
  threshold.
- **High-risk customers** — everyone currently scored above the risk
  threshold, as a table.

<Info>
Scores are produced by a churn model; the dashboard reads and acts on them.
Customer names are resolved where possible, with a short id as a fallback.
</Info>

## Open alerts

An alert is created when a customer's churn score **crosses the alert
threshold**. Each alert card shows:

- The **customer** (name, or short id).
- The score movement — **previous score → new score**, with the new score
  highlighted.
- The **alert type** badge.
- The **threshold** that was crossed and when the alert was raised.

### Acknowledge an alert

Click **Acknowledge** on a card to clear it from the open list once you've
acted on it (for example, reached out to the customer or triggered a save
offer). Acknowledged alerts drop off the board immediately.

<Info>
Acknowledging is a triage action — it records that the alert was seen and
handled. It doesn't change the customer's underlying score.
</Info>

## High-risk customers

The table lists everyone currently above the risk threshold:

| Column | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| Customer | Name, or short id |
| Risk score | The current numeric churn score |
| Level | `critical` / `high` (red), `medium` (amber), or `low` |
| Model | The scoring model version that produced the score |

Unlike alerts, this list isn't acknowledged away — it always reflects who is at
risk **right now**, so it's the place to prioritize retention outreach.
