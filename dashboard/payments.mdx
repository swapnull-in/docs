## Overview

Two dashboard screens cover money that moves outside the online checkout:

- **Offline payments** — record NEFT / cash / cheque receipts and issue
  **virtual accounts** customers transfer into.
- **Mandates** — **UPI Autopay** authorizations that let you debit a customer
  up to a cap each cycle.

<Info>
Virtual accounts and UPI mandates are **INR** features. Amounts are entered in
major units in the forms and stored as minor units.
</Info>

## Offline payments

Open **Offline payments** in the left nav. It has two tabs: **Recorded
payments** and **Virtual accounts**.

### Record a payment

On the **Recorded payments** tab, click **Record payment** to open a right-side
sheet:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Pick the customer and (optionally) an invoice">
    Choose a **Customer**. The **Invoice** picker then lists that customer's
    unpaid invoices (open / overdue / past due). Selecting one **settles that
    invoice** and prefills the amount and currency; leave it **Not linked** to
    record an unallocated payment.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the payment">
    Choose a **Type** — **Bank transfer**, **Cash**, or **Cheque** — and an
    **Amount**. Optionally add **TDS withheld**, a **Reference no.** (UTR /
    cheque number), and **Notes**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Record">
    Click **Record payment**. It appears in the table with the customer, type,
    amount (plus TDS if any), reference, linked invoice, and who recorded it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Virtual accounts

Switch to the **Virtual accounts** tab and click **New virtual account** to
issue a dedicated account number a customer can transfer into.

- Pick a **Customer**, optionally link an **Invoice** (which prefills the
  expected amount), and set the **Expected amount (INR)**.
- The table lists each account's number, IFSC and bank name, customer,
  **Expected** vs. **Received** amounts, and a **status** badge (**active** or
  otherwise).

<Info>
Recurso reconciles inbound bank transfers against the virtual account, moving
the **Received** amount up as money arrives.
</Info>

## UPI mandates

Open **Mandates** in the left nav. A mandate is a standing UPI Autopay
authorization to debit a customer up to a per-cycle cap.

Each row shows:

| Column | What it shows |
| --- | --- |
| **Customer** | The authorizing customer. |
| **VPA** | The customer's UPI ID. |
| **Max / cycle** | The per-cycle debit cap (INR). |
| **Frequency** | weekly, monthly, quarterly, or yearly. |
| **Status** | **created**, **authorized**, **active**, **paused**, or **revoked**. |
| **Next debit** | When the next automatic debit is scheduled. |

### Create a mandate

Click **New mandate** to open the sheet:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer and VPA">
    Pick a **Customer** and enter their **VPA (UPI ID)**, e.g.
    `customer@upi`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cap and frequency">
    Set the **Max amount (INR)** per cycle and a **Frequency** (weekly /
    monthly / quarterly / yearly).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Link a subscription (optional)">
    Optionally link one of the customer's non-canceled **subscriptions** so the
    mandate funds it. Click **Create mandate**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Revoke a mandate

Click **Revoke** on any row that isn't already revoked and confirm.

<Warning>
Revoking **stops all future automatic debits immediately** and can't be undone
— the customer must authorize a new mandate to resume auto-debit.
</Warning>
