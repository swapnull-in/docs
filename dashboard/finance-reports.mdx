## Overview

The finance reports turn your billing data into the numbers finance actually
files and reviews — how MRR moved, how revenue recognizes over time, whether
the books balance, what's overdue, and the tax returns for a period. Each
report is its own screen; this guide covers all of them.

<Info>
Most reports display in a **reporting currency** and, where amounts are summed
across schedules or accounts that may span currencies, fall back to showing
grouped **minor-unit** totals rather than asserting a single symbol. Several
reports need a few days of captured **MRR history** before they fill in.
</Info>

## MRR waterfall

**Route:** `/finance/mrr-waterfall`

Shows how monthly recurring revenue moved over a period you choose with the
**start/end date** pickers in the header. Three cards summarize **Starting
MRR**, **Net change** (with the percentage vs. start), and **Ending MRR**. When
there's starting MRR, two more cards show **Net Dollar Retention** (revenue kept
from existing customers, expansion included) and **Gross Dollar Retention**
(before expansion — churn and contraction only).

The floating-bar chart and the **Breakdown** list decompose the change into
**New**, **Expansion**, and **Reactivation** (additions, green) and
**Contraction** and **Churned** (reductions, red), bridging starting to ending
MRR.

<Warning>
If snapshots don't reach back to the start of your range, a banner notes that
everything present at the end is counted as **New**. Movement gets more accurate
each day as MRR history accrues.
</Warning>

## Revenue waterfall

**Route:** `/finance/revenue-waterfall`

The recognition curve plus the deferred-revenue rollforward. Cards show **Total
recognized** (booked as earned across all periods), **Total scheduled** (still
to recognize), and **Months on the curve**.

Pick a **month and year** in the header to see the **Deferred rollforward** for
that month — Opening + Added − Released = Closing — i.e. how the Deferred
Revenue balance moved. The **Recognition curve** table below lists each month
with its **Recognized** (historical) and **Scheduled** (future) revenue.

<Info>
The curve fills in once you invoice for a period — an annual plan, for example —
which creates the revenue schedule that spreads across its term.
</Info>

## Revenue recognition

**Route:** `/finance/revenue-recognition`

The deferred (unearned) revenue still on the books and when it's scheduled to
recognize, for a **month/year** you select. Cards show **Recognized** in the
chosen month, the **Deferred balance** still to recognize, and how many
**currencies** are deferred.

Two tables follow: the **Release schedule** (which future months the deferred
balance recognizes in, and how much) and **Deferred by currency** (the
still-deferred balance in each schedule's own currency).

<Info>
When more than one currency is deferred, the combined totals are shown in minor
units — the per-currency table carries the real, symboled numbers.
</Info>

## Revenue by plan

**Route:** `/finance/revenue-by-plan`

How recurring revenue splits across your plans. Cards show **Total MRR**,
**Plans with revenue**, and the **Top plan** (with its share of MRR). A ranked
bar list shows each plan's label, its share percentage, its MRR, and its
subscription count — largest first.

## Revenue by geography

**Route:** `/finance/revenue-by-geography`

The same ranked bar view as revenue by plan, but split by **customer country**.
Cards show **Total MRR**, the number of **Countries** with revenue, and the
**Top region**. Each bar shows a country's label, share percentage, MRR, and
subscription count.

## Trial balance

**Route:** `/finance/trial-balance`

Every account's posted totals, with the core double-entry invariant: **debits
must equal credits**. A banner at the top states whether the books balance and
whether any account carries a wrong-sign (abnormal) balance. Cards show **Total
debits**, **Total credits**, and a balanced/unbalanced **Status**.

The **Accounts** table lists each account with its code, name, type (Asset,
Liability, Equity, Revenue, Expense), debits, credits, and balance. Any account
whose balance sits on the wrong side is flagged **abnormal** in red.

Use **Export GL (CSV)** in the header to download the full general ledger.

<Warning>
An **Out of balance** banner (debits ≠ credits) or an abnormal-balance flag
means the ledger needs investigating — start with
[Reconciliation](/dashboard/finance-reports#reconciliation).
</Warning>

## Reconciliation

**Route:** `/finance/reconciliation`

An on-demand check that billing records, the Postgres ledger, and TigerBeetle
(when enabled) all agree. It runs when you open the page; **Run again** re-runs
it. Cards show **Invoices Checked** (with the paid subset), the number of
**Discrepancies**, the **TigerBeetle** comparison status (**Compared** with
account/transfer counts, or **Skipped** with a reason), and the **Last Run**
time.

If everything agrees, you get a **Books balanced** state. Otherwise a
**Discrepancies** table lists each issue by **type** — e.g. missing invoice
transaction, invoice/payment amount mismatch, orphaned transaction, or a
Postgres/TigerBeetle mismatch — with the invoice, transaction, and reference
IDs and the **Expected** vs. **Found** amounts (in minor units).

<Info>
If the result is truncated, a notice tells you how many discrepancies are shown
out of the total — resolve those and run again to see the rest.
</Info>

## Invoice aging

**Route:** `/finance/invoice-aging`

Outstanding receivables bucketed by how far past due each open invoice is. Cards
show **Total outstanding** (with the open-invoice count), **Overdue** (past due
date), and **Current** (not yet due).

The **By age** bar chart breaks the total into buckets — **Current**, **1–30**,
**31–60**, **61–90**, and **90+ days** — coloured from healthy green to deep
red as they age, each with its amount and invoice count.

## Unit economics

**Route:** `/finance/unit-economics`

Revenue per account and per subscription, plus lifetime value. Cards show
**ARPA** (average revenue per customer/month), **ARPU** (average revenue per
subscription/month), and **LTV**.

The **Basis** panel shows the inputs: **MRR**, **Active customers**, **Active
subscriptions**, and **Monthly churn**.

<Info>
LTV = ARPA ÷ monthly revenue churn. Churn is measured from captured MRR history,
so LTV shows `—` until there's a trailing month to compare against.
</Info>

## GST returns

**Route:** `/finance/gst-returns`

Return-ready India GST filings for a tax period. Set the **month** and **year**
(it defaults to the previous month — the period you'd be filing), then switch
between the **GSTR-1** (outward supplies) and **GSTR-3B** (summary) tabs.

Click **Build return** to generate and preview the return's sections. Once
built, download it as either the **GSTN JSON** (the exact upload file the GSTN
portal accepts) or a **Readable** JSON copy.

<Warning>
GST export must be configured on the deployment. If it isn't, building a return
reports that the export isn't available. See
[Set up GST e-invoicing](/dashboard/workflows/india-gst-invoicing).
</Warning>
