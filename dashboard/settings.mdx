## Overview

**Settings** holds your company's general information and links out to the tax
configuration screens. The main page has two parts:

- **General information** — your **company name** and **support email**. Edit them
  and click **Save changes**.
- A list of sub-pages: **Security**, **GST configuration**, **E-invoicing (IRP)**,
  and **US sales-tax nexus**.

<Info>
The **Security** link opens the [Security](/dashboard/team-security#security)
page (2FA and sessions).
</Info>

## GST configuration

Set the GST details Recurso stamps on Indian invoices. Open **Settings → GST
configuration**, then **Save configuration** when done.

<Steps>
  <Step title="GSTIN details">
    Enter your 15-character **GSTIN** and click **Validate**. On a valid number,
    the **state code**, **state name**, and **PAN** are auto-filled (state fields
    are read-only). You can also set PAN manually.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Business details">
    Fill in your **legal name** (as registered), **trade name**, and **registered
    address**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tax settings">
    Set the **SAC code** (defaults to `998314` for SaaS), the **GST rate**
    (standard software rate is 18%), and tick **LUT (Letter of Undertaking)** to
    apply 0% GST on export of services.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## E-invoicing (IRP)

Connect the NIC **Invoice Registration Portal** so Recurso can generate an IRN
for B2B invoices. Open **Settings → E-invoicing (IRP)**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable and choose environment">
    Toggle **Enable e-invoicing**, then pick the **environment** — **Sandbox**
    for testing or **Production**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter credentials">
    Provide your **GSTIN** and the NIC API **Client ID**, **Client secret**,
    **Username**, and **Password**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Test and save">
    Click **Test connection** to verify the credentials — a green or red banner
    reports the result — then **Save configuration**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Turning on e-invoicing without a successful connection test means B2B invoices
may fail to register an IRN. Test in **Sandbox** before switching to
**Production**.
</Warning>

## US sales-tax nexus

Open **Settings → US sales-tax nexus** to declare where you collect US sales tax
and to watch economic-nexus thresholds.

### Declared nexus states

Add a row per state: a two-letter **state code** and a **nexus type**
(**physical**, **voluntary**, or **economic**). **Save states** persists the list.

<Warning>
Saving an **empty** list clears all declared nexus.
</Warning>

### Economic-nexus status

A read-only table (scoped to the current year) shows, per state with activity:
**YTD taxable sales**, **YTD transactions**, and **threshold proximity** as a
percentage. A state that has crossed its threshold is flagged, and crossings
auto-establish nexus server-side.

<Info>
Economic-nexus tracking depends on the deployment — if it isn't available you'll
see a note instead of the table. When the threshold dataset hasn't passed
professional review, a banner marks the proximity figures as **indicative, not
advice**.
</Info>
