## Overview

Refunds and adjustments in Recurso are **credit notes**. You issue a credit note
for a customer — optionally linked to a specific invoice — and it either reduces
what they owe or triggers a refund back to their payment method. This workflow
issues the credit note from the **Credit notes** section and explains the
downstream effect at the gateway and in the double-entry ledger.

<Info>
Grab the invoice first. Linking the credit note to the paid invoice keeps the
refund tied to the original charge, which the ledger and reporting rely on.
</Info>

## Before you start

- Find the invoice you're refunding in **Invoices** and open its detail sheet to
  confirm the amount, currency, and that it's **Paid**. Note its ID.

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the credit note form">
    Go to **Credit notes** and click **Create credit note**. The form opens in a
    right-hand sheet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Fill in the credit">
    - **Customer** — pick from the dropdown (required).
    - **Credit amount** — the value to refund/credit, in major units (required).
    - **Linked invoice (optional)** — paste the invoice ID from the step above so
      the credit is tied to that charge.
    - **Reason** — free text, e.g. *Service downtime compensation*.

    Click **Issue credit note**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the credit note's status">
    The new note appears in the list with an **Amount**, a **Balance**
    (how much is still unused), and a **status** (`issued`, `used`, `active`).
    Click it to open the detail sheet, which also shows the **Reason** and, for a
    money-back refund, a **Refund status**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Understand the gateway + ledger effect">
    Depending on the credit type:

    - **Refund to the customer** — Recurso asks the payment gateway to refund the
      original charge; the note's **Refund status** tracks that request through to
      completion.
    - **Credit toward future invoices** — the balance drains against the
      customer's next invoices instead of moving money at the gateway.

    In both cases the credit note posts its own **double-entry legs** to the
    ledger, so the refund/adjustment is reflected in the trial balance and
    reconciliation — nothing is booked outside the books.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
A credit note's balance is consumed as it's applied — once a note is fully
**used** it can't be reused. Issue a new note for a further adjustment.
</Warning>
