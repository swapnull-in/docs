## Overview

Subscription revenue is earned over the service period, not when it's invoiced.
Recurso **defers** revenue on invoice and **recognizes** it month by month under
ASC 606 — giving you a recognition waterfall and a deferred-revenue rollforward
your auditor can use.

<Info>
Concept + posting detail: [Revenue Recognition](/advanced/revenue-recognition) and
[Ledger](/advanced/ledger).
</Info>

## Step 1: Nothing to switch on — it follows your plans

Recognition is driven by your plan/subscription terms: a monthly plan recognizes
over the month, an annual plan recognizes across twelve. Set your plan intervals
correctly and the schedule follows. One-off charges recognize at point of sale.

## Step 2: Read the recognition report

Pull the period's recognized revenue, the deferred balance, and the release
schedule. In the dashboard it's **Finance → Revenue recognition**; or via the API:

```bash
curl "https://api.recurso.dev/v1/finance/revrec/report?month=6&year=2026" \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $API_KEY"
```

The **waterfall** (`/finance/revrec/waterfall`) and the **deferred rollforward**
(`/ledger/deferred-rollforward`) give the auditor views.

## Step 3: Reconcile against the ledger

Recognition posts double-entry legs — deferred revenue down, recognized revenue
up — so the numbers tie to the ledger and the [month-end close](/setup/month-end-close).

## Verify your setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="Deferred revenue appears on invoice">
    Invoicing an annual plan defers the revenue rather than recognizing it all up
    front.
  </Step>
  <Step title="It releases over time">
    Each period recognizes its slice; the deferred balance rolls forward correctly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="It ties to the ledger">
    Recognized + deferred reconciles against the ledger's balances.
  </Step>
</Steps>
