## Overview

A quote is a proposed set of charges you send a customer for approval. They
accept (or decline); on acceptance you convert it into an invoice or
subscription. Quotes have an expiry and their own PDF.

<Info>
Full lifecycle: [Quotes](/advanced/quotes) · dashboard: [Quotes](/dashboard/quotes).
</Info>

## Step 1: Create the quote

In the dashboard, go to **Quotes → Create**; or via the API:

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.recurso.dev/v1/quotes \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "customer_id": "cus_...",
    "line_items": [{ "description": "Enterprise plan (annual)", "amount": 1200000 }],
    "expires_at": "2026-08-31"
  }'
```

## Step 2: Send it for acceptance

Send the quote to the customer — they review it (and its PDF) and **accept** or
**decline**. A quote past its `expires_at` can't be accepted.

## Step 3: Convert on acceptance

Once accepted, convert the quote into an invoice (or subscription). Optional
discounts on the quote carry through to the converted document.

## Verify your setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="The quote sends">
    The customer receives the quote and it shows as sent/pending.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Acceptance converts cleanly">
    Accepting produces an invoice/subscription with the quoted amounts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expiry is enforced">
    A quote past its expiry can no longer be accepted.
  </Step>
</Steps>
