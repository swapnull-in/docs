## Overview

India requires B2B invoices above the turnover threshold to be reported to the
**Invoice Registration Portal (IRP)**, which returns a signed **IRN** and a QR
code. Recurso submits finalized B2B invoices to the IRP automatically and retries
transient failures.

<Info>
This is India's IRN clearance — separate from [EU e-invoicing](/setup/eu-einvoicing).
Concept detail + statuses: [E-Invoicing (India)](/compliance/e-invoicing).
Assumes [GST is set up](/tutorials/india-gst-setup).
</Info>

## Step 1: Connect your IRP credentials

Provide your IRP/GSP connection. In the dashboard, go to **Settings → E-Invoicing
(IRP)**; or via the API:

```bash
curl -X PUT https://api.recurso.dev/v1/settings/irp \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer $API_KEY" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "client_id": "your_irp_client_id",
    "client_secret": "your_irp_client_secret",
    "username": "your_irp_username",
    "password": "your_irp_password",
    "gstin": "27AABCU9603R1ZM",
    "environment": "sandbox",
    "is_enabled": true
  }'
```

<Warning>
Test with `environment: "sandbox"` first — sandbox submissions are not reported to
the GST portal. Switch to `production` only once test invoices clear cleanly.
</Warning>

## Step 2: Keep customer + line data complete

The IRP rejects incomplete invoices. Make sure B2B customers have a valid
**GSTIN** and address, and that lines carry **HSN/SAC** codes — the most common
failures are a wrong buyer GSTIN or a missing field.

## Step 3: Finalize a B2B invoice

Once enabled, every finalized B2B invoice is submitted to the IRP; on success the
**IRN**, acknowledgement number, and signed **QR** are attached. Transient
failures are retried automatically with backoff.

## Verify your setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="The connection tests clean">
    Recurso can reach the IRP with your credentials (sandbox first).
  </Step>
  <Step title="A B2B invoice gets an IRN">
    A finalized B2B invoice shows e-invoice status `generated` with an IRN + QR.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Failures retry, not stick">
    A transient failure reschedules and recovers rather than staying failed.
  </Step>
</Steps>
