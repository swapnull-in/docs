## Overview

The customer portal is a self-service page where your customers sign in (magic
link) to update their payment method, view invoices, and manage their
subscription — without emailing support. The session is held in an httpOnly
cookie, never in local storage.

<Info>
Dashboard-side controls: [Customer portal](/dashboard/customer-portal) · overview:
[Portal](/portal/overview).
</Info>

## Step 1: Configure the portal

In the dashboard, go to the **Customer portal** settings and choose what customers
can do (update card, change plan, view invoices, cancel). Set your branding and
the return URL.

## Step 2: Make sure email + a gateway are set

The portal signs customers in by **magic link** (needs [email](/setup/notifications))
and lets them update cards (needs a [payment gateway](/setup/payment-gateway) — a
Stripe SetupIntent, or a UPI mandate re-auth).

## Step 3: Send customers to the portal

Link customers to the portal from your app or from billing emails. They request a
magic link, sign in, and self-serve.

## Verify your setup

<Steps>
  <Step title="A magic link signs in">
    A customer requests a link and lands in the portal, authenticated by cookie.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Card update works">
    Updating the payment method in the portal saves via the gateway.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Invoices are visible">
    The customer sees their invoice history.
  </Step>
</Steps>
